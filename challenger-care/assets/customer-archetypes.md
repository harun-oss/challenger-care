# Customer Archetypes — Challenger Care

The customers actually buying Challenger today, grounded in:
1. **The Preparer** persona from the [Brand Strategy](./brand-strategy.md) (Hayden's defined target)
2. **480 verbatim reviews** from JudgeMe spanning 2007–2026
3. **Demographic patterns** confirmed by VOC

Use these archetypes when writing any customer-facing copy — PDPs, emails, ads, social, Reddit posts. Every workflow that generates communication should ask: *which archetype am I writing to?*

> **Migration note:** v1 archetypes use JudgeMe corpus only (n=480). When the 500 Amazon reviews arrive, expect: clearer Amazon-vs-Shopify behavioral split, sharper subscriber segment, expanded sensitive-skin niche. Refresh quarterly.

## Archetype 1 · The Preparer (primary bullseye)

> *"I take the day seriously. My appearance is part of that."*

### Profile
- Men 28–45
- Working, dating, training, selling, interviewing, presenting, leading, traveling
- Optimizes everything — diet, fitness, work, sleep, finances
- Sees grooming as preparation, not vanity
- Time-poor · efficiency-driven
- Will pay premium for products that save him decisions

### What he says (verbatim from reviews)
- *"Holds my hair in place for most of the workday."*
- *"I cut my hair every 2 weeks, I workout a lot."*
- *"I have a job that I frequently sweat at. This holds up well."*
- *"I was impressed with how little I needed to use."*

### What he buys
- Clean Cream Pomade (3oz)
- Eventually: the 3-pack
- Eventually: a Recharge subscription

### What unlocks him
- The Preparation pillar — *"One minute. Every room."*
- Specific time/result claims — *"All-day hold without re-application"*
- Permission to care without beauty-coding

### How to write to him
- Direct, peer-to-peer, no education needed
- Lead with result, not ingredient
- Reference his use case: gym before work, client meetings, travel days
- CTA: *"Accept the challenge"* or *"Shop the routine"*

## Archetype 2 · The Switcher (the most-loyal segment)

> *"I tried everything else. I came back."*

### Profile
- 35–55, has been using men's grooming products for 10–20 years
- Sometimes a recent convert from drugstore brands (Axe, Suavecito, Old Spice)
- Sometimes from premium / barbershop brands (Hanz De Fuko, Sumotech, etc.)
- Specific complaints with prior products: greasy, heavy scent, doesn't last, breaks him out
- Once he finds Challenger, he stays — high LTV, high subscription propensity

### What he says (verbatim from reviews)
- *"Best I've used over a period of about 15 years of hair products."*
- *"I switched from Axe and have much less head acne since switching."*
- *"While I prefer sumotech for my purposes, the Challenger matte paste is a suitable lower cost alternative."*
- *"Other pomades I've tried are usually chalky."*
- *"I won't try anything else anymore."*

### What he buys
- Starts with single jar (skeptical)
- Returns within 110 days for a second jar
- Eventually a 3-pack or subscription

### What unlocks him
- Comparison-frame ads (vs. "petroleum pomades" per brand voice rules · or direct competitor names in Reddit founder voice)
- "Customer switch stories" landing page block
- Reviews-led PDPs ("what customers used before")

### How to write to him
- Comparison framing
- Anchor to specific past frustrations (grease, scent, didn't last)
- Use the language of switching — *"used to use," "switched from," "haven't gone back"*

## Archetype 3 · The Sensitive Buyer (high-loyalty niche)

> *"Everything else gives me a reaction. This doesn't."*

### Profile
- Skews older (40+) or has documented sensitivities
- Fragrance allergies · sensitive scalp · acne-prone skin · post-COVID smell disruption
- Has tried 5+ products before Challenger
- Often introduced to the brand by a partner or by Reddit / search
- Highest 5-star rate of any segment
- Highest LTV — once they find Challenger, they stop searching

### What he/she says (verbatim from reviews)
- *"I have severe fragrance allergies and searched to find a hair gel that would hold my spiked hair and not cause a reaction."*
- *"This is the only hair product my boyfriend can use without breaking out."*
- *"Hair products have always caused acne on my forehead… until finding this."*
- *"Almost all hair products cause her to smell 'wet dog.'"*
- *"I have very sensitive skin and hair. All pomades in stores contain fragrance. Finally found a pomade made for my hair and skin type."*

### What he/she buys
- Single jar of Clean Cream Pomade specifically (fragrance-free)
- Often reorders the same SKU repeatedly · less likely to expand to other products without trust signal
- Will pay premium

### What unlocks him/her
- The **"Pomade Acne"** acquisition hook — high-intent search demand currently untapped
- **Fragrance-free** as the lead message (not a secondary claim)
- Targeted communities: r/malegrooming, r/SkincareAddiction, r/Allergies, derm-related Instagram

### How to write to him/her
- Lead with **what's removed**: fragrance, sulfates, parabens, irritants
- Specific medical-adjacent language without making medical claims (per [Claim Library](./claim-library.md))
- Sensitive-skin testimonials in PDP
- "Non-comedogenic" used in plain language

## Archetype 4 · The Wife / Girlfriend / Mom (purchase influencer)

> *"He'll actually use this."*

### Profile
- Woman buying for husband, son, partner, or grown family member
- Amazon-dominant (gift purchase + reorder convenience)
- Reads more reviews than men do before buying
- Decides based on: will he like the scent (or lack of), is it easy to use, does it work, is the packaging OK
- Cross-shops with the women in her life as well

### What she says (verbatim from reviews)
- *"My 4-year-old son wanted to look like his daddy. I didn't want to just use anything on his hair."*
- *"My husband & I both love this matte cream pomade."*
- *"I bought this looking for a hair product for my husband that wasn't perfume-y."*
- *"This is a great shampoo marketed to men. I, however, am a woman with longer hair."*
- *"I ordered this for my college-age grandson because he wanted something without a strong scent."*

### What she buys
- Single jar gifts → larger gifts (combos, trios) → reorders
- Birthday / Father's Day / Christmas spikes
- Subscribe & Save on Amazon (very high attach)

### What unlocks her
- Gift / bundle / family-use framing on PDPs
- "He'll actually use this" angle
- Long-form reviews from women — already the strongest converters when displayed
- Amazon listing optimization for the gift-aisle scenario

### How to write to her
- PDP language must work for both: male voice for the user, gift-friendly framing for the buyer
- Per the brand book: *"Lead with male voice, but make it easy for women to recognize their man will use this"*
- Bundle copy: *"For the man in your life"* — works for Amazon, less for Shopify

## Archetype 5 · The Founder-Adjacent Discoverer (Reddit / community)

> *"A guy I trust online said this was the answer."*

### Profile
- 22–40, terminally online, Reddit-active
- Trusts community recommendations far more than ads
- Discovered Challenger via:
  - Hayden's $5 jar drops in r/malegrooming
  - Organic mentions in product-comparison threads
  - Founder-voice content
- High repeat behavior once converted · medium-to-high LTV
- Subscribe-on-Shopify (not Amazon) once converted

### What he says (verbatim)
- *"I searched all over for this and couldn't be more pleased."*
- *"Finally I've found something that works for me."*
- *"This is the answer to the pomade acne thing people complain about on Reddit."*
- *(Founder posts and AMA threads — verbatim style)*

### What he buys
- Tries single jar first (low-stakes test)
- Becomes evangelist if converted
- Will recommend to specific friends, post in threads, leave detailed reviews

### What unlocks him
- Authentic community presence (Hayden's existing pattern — $5 drops + helpful replies)
- Founder voice on Reddit (NOT brand voice — first-person, opinion-led)
- Long, specific product comparisons in threads
- No traditional ad creative — feels off in this channel

### How to write to him
- Founder voice (Hayden-style, not brand voice)
- Specific, detailed comparisons (where Challenger wins, where it doesn't)
- Reddit-native: no marketing speak, no "buy now" CTAs
- Earn trust before mentioning the brand

## Cross-archetype patterns

### Where each archetype concentrates

| Archetype | Amazon | Shopify | Subscribe |
|---|---|---|---|
| The Preparer | Medium | High | High |
| The Switcher | High | Medium | High |
| The Sensitive Buyer | Medium | Medium | Medium-high |
| The Wife / Mom | **Very high** | Low | High (S&S) |
| The Reddit Discoverer | Low | **High** | Medium-high |

### Acquisition hooks by archetype

| Archetype | Top hook |
|---|---|
| The Preparer | *"One minute. Every room."* — the brand platform |
| The Switcher | Comparison-framed *"better than X"* content |
| The Sensitive Buyer | *"Pomade acne"* / fragrance-free / non-comedogenic |
| The Wife / Mom | Gift bundles + "he'll actually use this" |
| The Reddit Discoverer | Founder voice + $5 jar drops + helpful threads |

### Retention drivers by archetype

| Archetype | What keeps him buying |
|---|---|
| The Preparer | Consistency · subscription convenience · the 3-pack as default |
| The Switcher | Doesn't fail · doesn't break · no reason to leave |
| The Sensitive Buyer | The product works for sensitivities others don't · stickiest segment |
| The Wife / Mom | Easy reorder + Amazon S&S |
| The Reddit Discoverer | Community presence · founder accessibility · feels like part of something |

## When this file should drive a workflow

Any workflow that generates customer-facing copy should ask:

1. **Which archetype is this for?** Pick one as primary, max one as secondary.
2. **What's their hook?** Pull from the archetype's "what unlocks" section.
3. **What's their language?** Borrow from the verbatim quotes for that archetype.
4. **What channel are they on?** Match the channel rules from [Brand Strategy](./brand-strategy.md).

If a piece of copy can't answer those four questions, it needs sharpening before it ships.
