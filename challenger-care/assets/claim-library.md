# Claim Library — Challenger Care

What Claude (and the team) can and cannot say across PDPs, emails, ads, social, support replies, and Reddit posts. Every claim here is either validated, in-use, or restricted.

---

## Validated claims · use freely

### Hold claims
- **"All-day hold"** — preferred phrasing
- **"9 to 12 hour hold"** — when specificity is appropriate (data-driven contexts)
- **"Holds without re-application"** — supported by review evidence
- **"Workout-safe hold"** — water-based formula tolerates sweat

### What we remove (the core differentiator)
- **"Fragrance-free"** — formulated without added fragrance
- **"Unscented"** — alternate phrasing, equally valid
- **"Sulfate-free"** — applies to shampoo and wash products
- **"No artificial dyes"** — accurate per ingredient deck
- **"No parabens"**
- **"No petroleum-based ingredients"**

### Formula descriptors
- **"Water-based"** — accurate, key differentiator vs petroleum styling products
- **"Non-comedogenic"** — formulated to not clog pores (plain language: won't cause breakouts in most users)
- **"Clean ingredients"** — supported by the formula

### Brand-level claims
- **"Proudly made in the USA"**
- **"For the everyman"**
- **"Designed for sensitive skin"** — when context is appropriate
- **"Cruelty-free"** — confirm with Emanuel before using on packaging or ads (likely true but document the supplier confirmation)

---

## Cautious claims · use with care

These work but need framing or context.

- **"Best hair styling product on the market"** — Hayden's direct claim, but only use in contexts where opinion framing is clear (founder voice, Reddit posts, AMAs). Don't use in headline ad copy as a flat claim.
- **"Most versatile"** — same framing rule.
- **"Conquer the morning"** / **"Confidence starts in the mirror"** — brand-level positioning lines, use as taglines not as product claims.

---

## Banned claims · do not use

### Medical / health
- **No acne treatment claims** — even though non-comedogenic is supported, do not say "clears acne," "treats acne," "prevents breakouts" without medical claim review.
- **No dermatologist-recommended language** unless we have a specific dermatologist endorsement on file.
- **No "cures," "heals," "treats"** anywhere.

### Hold guarantees
- **No "strongest hold ever"** — unsubstantiated.
- **No "lasts X+ days"** — exceeds product reality.
- **No "won't ever wash out"** — false.

### Comparative
- **No naming competitor brands negatively** in formal copy. OK in founder voice on Reddit / podcast / community contexts where opinion framing is clear.
- **Specific exception:** It's fine to position vs. "petroleum-based pomades" or "fragrance-heavy products" generically. Don't name Hanz De Fuko, Suavecito, etc. by name in ads.

### Superlatives
- **No "perfect," "flawless," "miracle"** — gimmicky and not on-brand.

## Tone & voice rules

### Approved tone
- Clean, confident, science-aware but not clinical
- Community-oriented, thoughtful — not bro
- Masculine but inclusive
- Quietly premium

### Avoided tone
- Bro-cliché ("crush it," "level up," "alpha")
- Overclaim hype ("game-changer," "revolutionary," "best ever")
- Vague wellness fluff ("self-care journey," "wellness ritual")
- Tech-speak for product claims ("proprietary blend," "patented technology" — unless literally true)

## Decision rules for Claude

When generating any customer-facing copy:

1. **Lead with what we remove**, not what we add — fragrance-free, sulfate-free, etc.
2. **Hold language is "all-day"** — not "long-lasting," not "all-day-and-night," not "lifetime."
3. **Never make medical claims** — anything related to skin, scalp, hair growth, dandruff, acne needs review.
4. **Comparative framing is generic** — "vs. petroleum pomades" yes, "vs. Hanz De Fuko" no.
5. **Specific numbers always need a source** — "9–12 hour hold" only if Hayden has confirmed the test data behind it.
