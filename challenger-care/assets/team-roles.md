## Permission tiers explained

Every workflow, alert, and AI action is tagged with one of three tiers.

### **Generate** (lowest risk · everyone can run)
- Drafts only · no live changes anywhere
- Outputs land in Drive, drafts, or chat
- Examples: Draft email · generate ad concept · run VOC analysis · build a report
- Reversible: Nothing to reverse — nothing changed

### **Stage** (medium risk · Ivey + above)
- Pre-loads content into a connected tool (Klaviyo, Shopify, Asana) but doesn't publish
- Requires explicit "ship" approval from someone with Execute permission
- Examples: Stage a Klaviyo flow · queue PDP changes · pre-load a campaign
- Reversible: Within 24 hours · easy to delete staged drafts

### **Execute** (highest risk · Hayden only initially)
- Live customer-facing change OR money out the door
- Examples: Send a campaign · pause a flow · place a PO · publish a PDP · approve a discount
- Reversible: Varies — some are reversible (un-pause), others aren't (campaign sent)
- Rollback windows documented per workflow

## Onboarding checklist for new team members

When adding a new person to the AI system:

1. Decide permission tier (Generate / Stage / Execute / mixed)
2. Add to Claude Project as member
3. Grant Drive folder access (read-only on knowledge, write on outputs)
4. Walk through dashboard + 5 most-used workflows
5. Confirm understanding of the three tiers (especially that Execute is destructive)
6. Pair them on their first 3 workflow runs before solo permissions
7. Update this doc with their entry
