## Strategic priorities (Jim's framework)

The order of work. Every initiative ladders to one of these.

2. **Conversion foundation** — PDP, hero, AOV path · build leverage points AI can compound against
3. **Paid acquisition** — Meta + Google · AI-run where possible
4. **Retention / LTV** — Klaviyo flows · subscription migration · SMS concierge
5. **TikTok Shop** — near-term move · content engine via Implicit

## What "success" looks like at handoff

From the stakeholder interview, Hayden's direct words on what makes the engagement worth $3K:

> *"Improved site communication of product strengths. Clarified mission/values. Improved product subscriber conversion. 20% lift in AOV. Infrastructure for AI management in Claude for Shopify, Klaviyo, Figma, Social, Quickbooks/other tools we need for Reporting and Recurring Tasks while we grow."*

And what he wants the system to be able to do at handoff:

1. **Pull actionable performance data** from Seller Central, Shopify, Klaviyo for analysis
2. **Generate concepts + designs** for creative (static ads, website creative, emails, collection + product pages)
3. **Enable a marketing associate to leverage tools/connectors** without causing damage (intentionally or otherwise)

If those three things work at handoff, the engagement was worth it.

## Decision rules for Claude

When proposing initiatives, prioritizing work, or evaluating opportunities:

1. **AOV is the gate** — until Shopify AOV hits $40+, paid acquisition is constrained
2. **Subscription is the LTV play** — every offer should have a subscription tier
3. **Email revenue is the cheap money** — capture before chasing paid
4. **TikTok Shop is the content engine** — feeds everything downstream
5. **Amazon is the cash engine, not the growth lever** — maintain, don't optimize
6. **Hayden's time is the constraint** — workflows that save him hours beat workflows that save him minutes
