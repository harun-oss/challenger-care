# Competitive Analysis Framework — Patterns, Gaps, and Strategic Implications

## The Goal of Competitive Creative Research

The competitive ad audit is not about copying competitors. It's about understanding the creative landscape so Challenger can:
1. Avoid wasting budget on approaches that the market has already saturated
2. Identify gaps where differentiation is possible
3. Borrow proven formats and adapt them to Challenger's brand and positioning
4. Understand what baseline performance looks like in the category

---

## Pattern Recognition Framework

### Level 1: Format Patterns

What ad formats are dominant in the category?

| Observation | Implication |
|---|---|
| All competitors use static images | Video may stand out — test it |
| All competitors use UGC video | Try polished brand video to differentiate — or use better UGC |
| Carousel is dominant | Competitors may be using catalogue ads — verify if this is DPA |
| Text-heavy creatives | Audience is text-receptive; test copy-led formats |

### Level 2: Hook Patterns

What hook types appear most frequently?

| Observation | Implication |
|---|---|
| All hooks lead with price/discount | Price competition is high — find a non-price differentiator |
| All hooks lead with the product | Try leading with the problem or outcome instead |
| Social proof hooks dominate | High skepticism category — client needs strong social proof too |
| Question-based hooks common | Audience is pain-aware — lean into problem-solution framing |

### Level 3: Claim Patterns

What are competitors claiming most?

| Common claims | Better client approach |
|---|---|
| "Best quality" / "Premium" | Too generic — find a specific proof point |
| Generic "Save time/money" | Quantify — "Save 4 hours per week" |
| Founder credentials | Test only if credentials are genuinely differentiating |

## Gap Identification Framework

A gap is anything that the competitive set is collectively *not* doing, that could be an opportunity for Challenger.

### Format Gaps
- No video in a category where video could tell a compelling story
- No carousels showing the product range
- No UGC when the category would benefit from real customer voices
- No educational content when the product has a learning curve

### Angle Gaps
- No before/after transformation content when the product delivers visible results
- No community/identity framing when there's a passionate customer base
- No transparency content (behind the scenes, how it's made) when trust is a barrier
- No comparison content when Challenger has a genuine advantage over a named competitor

### Audience Gaps
- Competitors targeting a specific demographic exclusively when an adjacent one is underserved
- All B2C messaging when B2B use case exists (or vice versa)
- All English-language content when the market has a significant non-English speaking segment

## Scoring Competitor Creative

When analysing individual ads, score them on these dimensions to identify what's working:

**Hook strength (1–3):**
- 1 = Generic or unclear ("Introducing [Brand]")
- 2 = Relevant but not compelling ("Check out our [product]")
- 3 = Strong pattern interrupt or emotional trigger ("This is why I never go back to [alternative]")

**Offer clarity (1–3):**
- 1 = No offer or unclear offer
- 2 = Offer present but buried
- 3 = Offer is the headline — immediately clear

**Visual quality (1–3):**
- 1 = Low-quality, cluttered, or off-brand
- 2 = Competent but unremarkable
- 3 = High-quality, on-brand, visually stops the scroll

**Longevity signal:**
- 7–14 days: testing
- 30–59 days: performing
- 60+ days: strong performer

## Translating Research into Client Recommendations

Every competitive audit should end with specific recommendations for Challenger's first experiments. The format:

**Borrow and adapt:** "Competitor X has been running [format] for 60+ days — this is a proven format in the category. Adapt it to [Client]'s brand with [specific difference]."

**Fill the gap:** "No competitor is using [angle/format]. This represents a differentiation opportunity. Test [specific approach] in Experiment #1."

**Avoid the crowded space:** "All 4 competitors are leading with price discounts. For [Client], we recommend avoiding discount-led creative until we've established performance benchmarks with value-led creative."

**Upgrade what's weak:** "Competitor hooks are generic ('Introducing [Brand]'). A strong problem-led or outcome-led hook from [Client] should outperform — test [specific hook example]."
