# Value Prop Research Guide — AI Prompts & Research Methods

## Using AI to Accelerate Research

The SOP explicitly recommends using AI (ChatGPT, Claude) for the Value Prop Exercise. Here are the most effective prompts and methods.

---

## AI Prompt Templates

### Prompt 1: Brand Analysis from Website

```
Analyse the following website and answer these questions:
1. What is the primary product or service being offered?
2. What problem does it claim to solve?
3. What is the tone of the brand? (premium, friendly, clinical, bold, minimal)
4. What does the homepage lead with? (features, outcomes, lifestyle, social proof)
5. What are 3 potential unique differentiators based on what you see?
6. Who is the likely target customer? (demographics, psychographics)

Website: [URL]
```

### Prompt 2: Review Mining Summary

```
Here are [N] customer reviews for [Brand]. Please:
1. Identify the top 3 themes of what customers love most
2. Identify the top 3 pain points customers had before buying
3. Identify any recurring objections or hesitations mentioned
4. Pull 3-5 direct quotes that could work as ad headline language
5. Identify 2-3 personas based on the review language

Reviews:
[paste reviews]
```

### Prompt 3: Competitor Positioning Comparison

```
Compare the following brands on these dimensions: price point, tone, differentiators, target customer, and primary ad angle.

Brand A: [Client brand + URL]
Brand B: [Competitor 1 + URL]
Brand C: [Competitor 2 + URL]

For [Client brand], what positioning gaps or white-space opportunities do you see vs. competitors?
```

### Prompt 4: Messaging Angle Generation

```
Based on this brand profile, generate 6 distinct ad messaging angles for Meta/Facebook ads. For each angle:
1. Name the angle type (problem-solution, social proof, identity, etc.)
2. Write a sample headline (under 40 characters)
3. Write a sample first line of primary text (1 sentence)
4. Describe who this angle is best for

Brand: [Client name]
Product: [Product description]
Core value prop: [Key differentiator]
Target customer: [Persona description]
Top pain point: [Main problem they solve]
```

## Research Methods by Data Source

### Customer Reviews (Best Source)

**Where to find reviews:**
- Shopify product pages (scroll to reviews section)
- Google My Business reviews (Google the brand name + "reviews")
- Trustpilot: trustpilot.com/review/[domain]
- Amazon (if they sell on Amazon)
- Facebook page reviews
- Reddit mentions (search r/[category] for brand mentions)
- App Store / Google Play (for software/apps)

**How many to collect:** Aim for 20–50 reviews minimum. More is better. Focus on 4-star and 3-star reviews — 5-star reviews are often generic; lower stars reveal objections and pain points.

**What to look for:**
- Exact phrases customers use to describe their problem (these become ad headlines)
- Outcomes described in emotional terms ("I finally feel confident again")
- Comparisons to previous solutions ("I tried X and Y before, but this is different because...")
- Product attributes mentioned repeatedly (speed, quality, taste, ease — whatever keeps coming up)

### Website Copy

Read the full homepage and key landing pages:
- Headline and subheadline — what is the brand's primary claim?
- Features vs. benefits language — do they lead with features or outcomes?
- Social proof placement — how prominent is it, and what form does it take?
- CTA language — what action are they asking for?
- FAQ section — often reveals the top customer objections

### Client Workbook

The Client Workbook should contain:
- Challenger's self-described target customer
- Their perceived unique selling points
- Previous marketing approaches and what worked/didn't
- Brand voice guidelines

Cross-reference Challenger's self-perception against what reviews and website copy actually show — there's often a gap.

### Competitor Website Review

Visit 2–3 competitor sites and note:
- What positioning do they lead with?
- What are they *not* saying? (gap that Challenger can own)
- Are they more expensive or cheaper?
- Do they have more or less social proof?

## Quality Check for Research

Before finalising the value prop document, verify:

**Is the research real?**
Every claim in the personas, differentiators, and messaging angles should trace back to a real source (a review quote, website copy, or client input). If you can't source a claim, it's speculation — remove it or label it as a hypothesis.

**Are the personas distinct?**
If your 3 personas would all respond to the same ad, they're not distinct enough. Each persona should have a different primary pain point or emotional driver.

**Are the differentiators genuine?**
The test: "Could this exact statement describe a major competitor?" If yes, it's a category claim, not a differentiator. Push for something more specific.

**Are the messaging angles testable?**
Each angle should represent a distinct psychological appeal. If two angles feel similar, merge them. The goal is to have angles that could genuinely produce different creative approaches.
