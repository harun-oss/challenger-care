# Messaging Angles Framework — by Vertical

## Universal Angles (Work Across Verticals)

### 1. Problem → Solution
**Best for:** Customers who are actively aware of their problem but don't know about this product
**Structure:** Open with the pain, agitate briefly, introduce the product as the relief
**Example:** "Still wasting hours on [pain point]? [Brand] cuts it to 15 minutes."
**When not to use:** For aspirational or luxury products where leading with pain feels off-brand

### 2. Social Proof / Numbers
**Best for:** Skeptical buyers; high-ticket products; markets with trust issues
**Structure:** Lead with a volume or quality signal, then the key outcome
**Example:** "47,000 customers switched to [Brand] this year. Here's why."
**When not to use:** When the brand is genuinely new and doesn't have meaningful numbers yet

### 3. Before / After (Transformation)
**Best for:** Products with a clear transformation outcome (fitness, beauty, software, productivity)
**Structure:** Establish the before state, then contrast with the after state
**Example:** "Before: 3 hours on spreadsheets. After: 15-minute automated report."
**When not to use:** When the transformation is subtle or hard to visualise

### 4. Objection Handling
**Best for:** High-consideration, high-ticket products; competitive markets
**Structure:** Name the objection the customer has, then reframe it
**Example:** "You don't need to [expensive/difficult thing]. [Brand] does it for you."
**When not to use:** When the objection hasn't actually been surfaced in research — never assume objections

### 5. Identity / Tribe
**Best for:** Lifestyle products, community-driven brands, niche audiences
**Structure:** Describe the type of person who uses this product
**Example:** "For people who take their [activity] seriously."
**When not to use:** For B2B or clinical/functional products where identity isn't the driver

### 6. Specificity / Data
**Best for:** B2B, tech, health, performance products where proof matters
**Structure:** Lead with a specific, credible metric
**Example:** "Reduce [specific pain point] by 40% in 90 days — guaranteed."
**When not to use:** When you don't have real data to back the claim

## Angles by Vertical

### eCommerce (Physical Products)

| Angle | Example Hook | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Quality/Materials | "Made with [unique material]. Built to last." | Works for premium positioning |
| Convenience | "Delivered in 2 days. Returns are free." | Lower barrier for first purchase |
| Gift/Occasion | "The gift they actually want." | Seasonal — increase spend during gift seasons |
| Bundle/Value | "Get [X] for the price of [Y]." | High volume, lower AOV |
| Founder/Story | "Started in [founder's kitchen] in 2019." | Trust and authenticity signal |
| UGC-style | First-person voice, informal | "I've been using this for 6 months and..." |

### Lead Gen / B2B SaaS

| Angle | Example Hook | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Time Savings | "Cut [task] from 4 hours to 20 minutes." | Always specific — don't say "save time" |
| Revenue / ROI | "Our clients see [X]% lift in [metric]." | Requires real data — use conservatively |
| Competitor Positioning | "Switch from [well-known tool] to [Brand]." | Bold — use only if you can back it up |
| Pain Point + Tool | "[Job role] use [Brand] to stop [specific pain]." | Persona-specific |
| Demo / Trial Offer | "See how [Brand] works in 15 minutes." | Low commitment CTA |
| Case Study | "How [Company type] increased [metric] by [%]." | Works well for re-targeting |

### Health / Wellness

| Angle | Example Hook | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Clinical/Proof | "[X]% of users reported [outcome] in [time]." | Always add disclaimers; Meta scrutinises health claims |
| Natural/Clean | "No [bad ingredient]. Ever." | Works for clean-label positioning |
| Routine/Consistency | "Used by [N]+ people every morning." | Habitual use framing |
| Doctor/Expert | "Formulated by [credential]." | Trust signal — verify the claim |
| Before/After | "My [symptom] improved in 2 weeks." | UGC style; check Meta health ad policies carefully |

## Angle Prioritisation Framework

After generating 5–6 angles, rank them for first testing priority:

**Test first:** Problem → Solution and Social Proof are almost always the highest-volume performers for new accounts. Run these first to establish a baseline.

**Test second:** Before/After and Identity angles. These tend to work well with warm audiences (retargeting, lookalikes).

**Test later:** Objection handling and Specificity/Data angles. These are more niche but can be highly effective for skeptical buyers.

**Don't test simultaneously:** Test 2–3 angles in the first experiment, not all 6. You need enough data per angle to read results.
