## Structured Copy Generation

### Category-by-Category Formula Examples

Use these when generating the 55 headlines in Phase 4D. Each category has a distinct purpose and tone.

**Category 1: Keyword Headlines (5 headlines, 20–30 chars)**

These contain the exact or close-variant target keyword. Always write at least 5 so the operator has options for different ad groups.

Format: `[Keyword]` or `[Keyword] [Qualifier]`

Examples:
- "Google Ads Management" (24 chars)
- "Shopify SEO Services" (20 chars)
- "Personal Injury Lawyer" (22 chars)
- "Commercial Cleaning NYC" (23 chars)
- "Credit Repair Services" (22 chars)

**Category 2: Angle / Features / Benefits (20 headlines, 20–30 chars)**

The largest category — write 20 distinct headlines covering different value angles. Mix emotional and rational.

Sub-types to cover across the 20:
- *Outcome headlines* (what the customer gets): "Stop Overpaying for Ads", "Skin That Glows Daily"
- *Feature headlines* (what makes it different): "No Long-Term Contracts", "Real-Time Inventory Sync"
- *Speed/ease headlines*: "Set Up in Under an Hour", "Results in 30 Days"
- *Comparison headlines* (vs. competitors or the status quo): "Better Than DIY — Proven"
- *Specific benefit*: "Cut CPA by 40% on Average", "Free Shipping Both Ways"

**Category 3: Pre-Qualification / Location (10 headlines, 20–30 chars)**

Filter the right audience in, filter the wrong audience out.

Sub-types:
- *Location*: "Serving Minneapolis Area", "NYC & Tri-State Region"
- *Business type*: "For Shopify Stores Only", "Built for DTC Brands"
- *Budget/scale qualifier*: "For Budgets $5K+/Month", "Enterprise Pricing Available"
- *Consumer qualifier*: "Homeowners in Minnesota", "For Pet Owners Who Care"

**Category 4: Price / Promotion / Offer / Guarantee (10 headlines, 20–30 chars)**

Specific and concrete — avoid vague offers.

Sub-types:
- *Price signal*: "Starting at $49/Month", "From $299 — No Hidden Fees"
- *Promotion*: "30% Off — This Week Only", "Buy 2 Get 1 Free"
- *Guarantee*: "30-Day Money-Back Promise", "Results or Your Money Back"
- *Free offer*: "Free Audit — No Commitment", "Free 14-Day Trial — No Card"

**Category 5: Call to Action (10 headlines, 20–30 chars)**

Specific CTAs only — not "Learn More" or "Click Here."

Sub-types:
- *Consultation/quote*: "Get a Free Quote Today", "Book a Free Strategy Call"
- *Start/trial*: "Start Your Free Trial Now", "Sign Up in 2 Minutes"
- *Urgency CTA*: "Limited Spots — Book Now", "Claim Your Offer Today"
- *Action verb + benefit*: "Save Now — Shop the Sale", "Call Now — Same-Day Service"

### Character Counting Reference

30-character examples (at the exact limit):

| Headline | Char count |
|----------|-----------|
| "Personal Injury Lawyer NYC" | 26 |
| "Free Shipping — Free Returns" | 29 |
| "No Long-Term Contracts Ever" | 27 |
| "Start Your Free Trial Today" | 28 |
| "Results in 30 Days Guaranteed" | 30 ✅ exact limit |
| "Results in 30 Days, Guaranteed" | 31 ❌ over limit |

Count spaces. A common mistake: writers count words and assume character count. Always count each character.

### Vertical Quick Reference

When writing headlines for a specific vertical, lead with these categories:

| Vertical | Lead with | Secondary | Avoid |
|---------|-----------|-----------|-------|
| Home Services (HVAC, Plumbing) | Location + Keyword | Speed/availability | Generic features |
| Legal | No-risk offer + Keyword | Experience signal | Price headlines |
| SaaS / Software | Keyword + differentiator | Free trial | Location |
| eCommerce DTC | Keyword + shipping | Social proof | Legal/compliance language |
| Health / Wellness | Benefit outcome | Guarantee | Jargon or clinical terms |
| Finance / Insurance | Trust signal | Guarantee | Urgency (can feel predatory) |

## The Core Principle: Relevance Over Strength

Google grades RSAs with an Ad Strength score (Poor → Good → Excellent). The score is a useful guide, but it optimizes for variety and keyword coverage — not necessarily for what actually converts.

**Rule: never sacrifice ad relevance for ad strength.**

A "Good" RSA where Headline 1 is pinned to the exact target keyword will outperform an "Excellent" RSA with a rotating Headline 1 that occasionally serves a generic phrase.

Use Ad Strength as a checklist to ensure you're covering enough headline variety, but always protect:
1. Pinned Headline 1 = target keyword (even though pinning reduces Ad Strength rating)
2. High-specificity benefit headlines over generic ones

## Headline Architecture

### Position 1 (Always Pinned)

**Rule:** Contains the exact target keyword or a very close variant.

Purpose: Headline 1 is the most prominent position in a Google Search ad. It's what the searcher reads first and what Google uses to assess ad relevance. Pinning a keyword-specific headline here ensures "Above average" ad relevance score.

**Format:** `[Service/Product] [Location]` or `[Service] [Qualifier]`

Examples:
- "Emergency HVAC Repair Minneapolis" (for ad group: emergency HVAC repair Minneapolis)
- "Commercial Cleaning Services" (for ad group: commercial cleaning services)
- "Personal Injury Lawyer Chicago" (for ad group: personal injury lawyer Chicago)
- "Shopify Inventory Management App" (for ad group: Shopify inventory management)

### Position 2 (Value Proposition)

The primary differentiator or benefit. Why choose this client over competitors?

**Formula options:**

| Formula | Example |
|---------|---------|
| [Result] + [Timeframe] | "Same-Day Service Guaranteed" |
| [Number] + [Proof Point] | "500+ 5-Star Google Reviews" |
| [Years] + [Credibility] | "Trusted in Minneapolis Since 2008" |
| [Unique Offer] | "Free 30-Min Consultation" |
| [Pain Removal] | "No Long-Term Contracts Required" |

### Position 3 (Call to Action or Location Signal)

For lead gen: a specific, action-oriented CTA.
For eCommerce: shipping, return policy, or price signal.
For local: city or regional qualifier if not already in Headline 1.

Examples:
- "Get a Free Quote Today"
- "Call Now — 24/7 Availability"
- "Ships Same Day — Free Returns"
- "Serving the Twin Cities Area"

### Positions 4–15 (Rotation Pool)

These headlines rotate to fill Headline 2 and 3 positions when they're not pinned. Write a variety:

**Benefit headlines:**
- Lead with an outcome the customer gets: "Cut Your Energy Bills Up to 30%", "Spotless Office Every Morning", "Settle Faster — Keep More Compensation"

**Trust signals:**
- "Licensed & Insured in Minnesota"
- "BBB Accredited, A+ Rating"
- "Google Guaranteed Partner"
- "10,000+ Clients Served"

**Urgency or scarcity (use only if genuine):**
- "Book Your Spot Before End of Month"
- "Limited Openings This Quarter"

**Offer-based:**
- "First Month Free for New Clients"
- "Free Initial Consultation — No Obligation"

**Feature + benefit:**
- "Eco-Friendly Products — Safe for Your Team"
- "Flat-Rate Pricing — No Surprise Invoices"

## Description Structure

**4 descriptions available; Google rotates to find the best-performing combination with the headlines.**

### Description 1: Problem → Solution

Lead with the pain or desire the searcher has. Follow with how Challenger solves it.

Format: `[Searcher's problem/desire]. [How client solves it].`

Examples:
- "Unexpected HVAC breakdown? Our licensed technicians are available 24/7 and can have your system running within hours. Call for same-day service."
- "Can't keep up with Shopify inventory across multiple channels? Automate stock sync in real time and stop worrying about overselling. Free 14-day trial."

### Description 2: Proof + CTA

Reinforce with social proof or credibility, then close with a clear action.

Format: `[Proof element] + [Specific CTA].`

Examples:
- "Over 500 five-star reviews on Google. Trusted by Minneapolis homeowners since 2003. Call us now for a free estimate — no obligation."
- "Join 5,000+ Shopify merchants who've eliminated inventory headaches. Start your free trial today — no credit card needed."

### Description 3 (optional): Offer or Differentiator

Highlight a specific offer, guarantee, or differentiator not covered in Descriptions 1 or 2.

### Description 4 (optional): Feature List or Specifics

For more complex products/services, list key features or what's included in the service.

## Common Ad Copy Mistakes

| Mistake | Why it's a problem | Fix |
|---------|-------------------|-----|
| Headline 1 not pinned | Generic headline in position 1 lowers ad relevance score | Pin keyword headline to position 1 |
| All headlines are about the same thing | Google has nothing to mix and match | Diversify: some benefit, some proof, some CTA, some feature |
| CTA is generic ("Learn More", "Click Here") | Doesn't tell the user what they're getting | Be specific: "Get Your Free Quote", "Start Your Free Trial", "Book a Same-Day Visit" |
| Description doesn't mirror the ad's promise | User clicks expecting X, landing page delivers Y | Review description → headline → landing page as a chain: all must be consistent |
| No location in local service ads | Generic national-sounding ad competes poorly against local-specific competitors | Include city name in at least one headline (H1 or H3) |
| Ad Strength "Excellent" but all headlines are generic | Ad Strength rewarded variety, not relevance | Remove generic headlines; replace with specific benefit headlines even if Ad Strength drops to "Good" |
