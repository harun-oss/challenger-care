## Section 3: General Filming Guidelines

Write this in second person, direct to the creator. Example:

> Talk directly to camera with a close, natural shot. Show yourself wearing (or using) the product at your desk, on your phone, or while reading. Add B-roll clips of real-life use — laptop screen, book in hand, scrolling on your phone, morning routine, etc.
>
> The goal is to feel like advice from a friend, not a polished ad. Keep it real, keep it close, keep it personal.

**For multiple concepts:** Produce a separate concept card for each. Use this format:

```
---
CONCEPT [N]: [Short concept name, e.g. "The Problem Hook"]

Hook direction: [What the creator should open with — the first 3 seconds]
Core narrative: [What to cover in the middle — 2–3 bullet points, not a script]
CTA closing line: [How to end — the exact call to action]

Scene / setting: [Where to film — home office, kitchen, coffee shop, etc.]
Tone: [Relatable / Authoritative / Energetic / Calm / Conversational]
Product moment: [When and how to show the product — e.g. "Put it on in the first 10 seconds"]
B-roll: [Specific cutaway shots needed]
---
```

## Section 4: About the Brand

Write in 2–3 short paragraphs. Plain language, no marketing jargon. Cover:
- Who founded the brand and why
- The core problem the product solves
- Why this solution is different from what already exists

**Language guide:** Write as if explaining to a friend, not pitching to an investor. Avoid words like "revolutionary", "game-changing", "innovative". Use words like "designed for", "built to", "created because".

---

## Section 5: Product Benefits

Use a bulleted list. Each bullet should be one specific, concrete benefit — not a vague claim.

✅ Good: "Reduces the eye strain that causes afternoon headaches after hours on a laptop"
❌ Avoid: "Improves your overall wellness and productivity"

---

## Section 6: What Makes It Different

2–4 sentences or a short bulleted list. Focus on the mechanism — *how* this product achieves what others don't. Reference the closest alternatives and explain the gap.

---

## Section 7: Key Messages to Communicate

Numbered list of 4–6 non-negotiables. These are the things the creator must convey regardless of which concept they film. Frame them as what the viewer should take away, not what the creator should say word-for-word.

Example format:
```
1. This product goes beyond blue light filtering — it targets the muscle fatigue behind eye strain
2. It was designed by an eye doctor, not a fashion brand
3. 8 out of 10 wearers felt real relief in a clinical study
4. No prescription needed — just wear them during screen time
5. Risk-free: free shipping, free returns, 7-day money-back guarantee
```

---

## Section 8: Do's and Don'ts

Present in two columns or clearly labelled blocks:

```
✅ DO
• [Specific action or message to include]
• [Specific action or message to include]
• [Specific action or message to include]

🚫 DON'T
• [Specific restriction — include the reason where helpful]
• [Specific restriction]
• [Claim restriction — e.g. "Don't position as a medical device or treatment"]
```

Always include at minimum:
- One "do" about authenticity / personal experience
- One "do" about the call to action
- One "don't" about absolute guarantees ("frame benefits as typical experiences, not guaranteed results")
- One "don't" about any category-specific claim restriction
