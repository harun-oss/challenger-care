# Content Rights & Usage Guide

## Standard Content Rights Clause

Copy the clause below into Section 11 of every creator brief. Fill in [Brand Name] and adjust any bespoke terms agreed with the creator before sending.

---

### Standard Clause

**Content Rights & Usage**

By delivering footage as part of this brief, you grant [Brand Name] and a non-exclusive, royalty-free, worldwide licence to use, edit, reproduce, distribute, and display the content across paid advertising channels (Meta, TikTok, Google, YouTube, Pinterest, and any other paid placements), organic social media, email, and the brand's website and marketing materials.

This licence is granted for an unlimited duration unless a fixed usage period has been agreed separately in writing.

retains the right to edit, crop, add captions, overlay text, add music, and repurpose the footage into multiple creative variants. You will be credited as the creator in organic posts where applicable, but not in paid ads (standard industry practice).

You confirm that:
- The footage is original and does not infringe on any third-party rights
- You have the right to appear in and grant usage of the footage
- No third-party music, logos, or trademarks appear in the footage (unless cleared in advance)
- You are over 18 years of age

---

## Revision Policy

| Round | What's included |
|-------|----------------|
| Round 1 | Creator delivers raw footage per the brief |
| Round 2 | If footage does not meet brief requirements (wrong orientation, missing key messages, technical quality issues), creator re-shoots the specific clips requested — at no extra charge |
| Round 3+ | Additional rounds beyond 2 are at the operator's discretion; significant deviations from the brief may require a new agreement |

**Important:** Revision requests are limited to brief compliance issues, not creative preference changes made after delivery. If changes the brief after footage is delivered, an additional round is provided at no charge.

---

## Approval Process

1. Creator delivers raw footage to the link provided in the brief
2. reviews within **3 business days** of receiving all files
3. If footage meets the brief: AM confirms receipt and brief is complete
4. If footage has issues: AM sends specific written feedback within 3 business days
5. Creator re-delivers revised clips within **5 business days** of receiving feedback

If the creator is posting content themselves:
1. Creator shares draft caption and video file with AM for approval **before posting**
2. AM approves or requests edits within **2 business days**
3. Creator posts only after receiving written approval

## Exclusivity (if applicable)

If the brand requires exclusivity (creator cannot post for direct competitors during a set period), this must be agreed separately in the creator contract — it is not covered by this standard brief. Flag to the operator before sending the brief if exclusivity is expected.

Common exclusivity windows: 30 days / 60 days / 90 days from content delivery or first posting date.

---

## Notes for the operator

- This clause is a standard starting point. For higher-value creator partnerships or long-term ambassadors, a formal creator agreement should be drafted by the brand's legal team.
- The standard clause covers paid usage only. If the brand wants to use footage in packaging, retail, or out-of-home advertising, that must be agreed separately.
- Always confirm that the creator has read and acknowledged the rights clause before sending payment or going live with the content.
