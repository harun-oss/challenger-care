# CTA Copy Library — Proven Formulas by Vertical and Action

## The CTA copy principle

Strong CTAs combine an **action verb** + **customer outcome**. The user should know exactly what they'll receive by clicking, and it should feel like a low-risk, high-reward exchange.

**Formula:** `[Action Verb] + [What They Get / Where They Go]`

Avoid: Submit · Click Here · Learn More · Get Started (alone) · Continue · Proceed

These are directional verbs with no destination — they tell users to do something but not what they'll get.

## By vertical

### B2C Lead-gen (payment processing, insurance, financial services)

Key principles: Lead with the benefit (saving money), reduce perceived commitment, use competitive framing.

Top-performing formulas:
- "Get My Free [Type] Analysis" — personalized, low-commitment
- "See How Much I'm Overpaying" — loss-framing, high curiosity
- "Compare [X] Options in [Timeframe]" — social proof + speed
- "Find My Best [Rate / Plan / Option]" — personalization
- "Talk to a Real Person" — for high-anxiety verticals where human contact reduces fear

Avoid: "Submit" (feels like junk mail), "Get Started" alone (vague), "Learn More" (not a conversion action)

---

### B2B Lead-gen (commercial real estate, SaaS, professional services)

Key principles: Professional tone, specific outcome, reduce commitment signals ("no obligation," "free," "15 minutes").

Top-performing formulas:
- "Schedule a Free [X]-Minute [Action]" — timeframe + no-cost framing
- "See [Specific Outcome] — No Obligation" — specificity + zero risk
- "Find a [Location / Solution] Near You" — localized relevance
- "Talk to [Role Title]" — humanizes the interaction
- "Get a Custom [Quote / Plan / Assessment]" — personalization

Avoid: "Contact Us" (generic), "Learn More" (no clear next step), "Reach Out" (too casual)

---

### eCommerce DTC

Key principles: Urgency, social proof, identity language, reduce perceived purchase risk.

Top-performing formulas:
- "Get Yours — Free Shipping" — action + immediate benefit
- "Add to Bag / Cart" (standard — fine) with a trust line below
- "Shop the Collection" — discovery framing for browse-mode users
- "Build Mine" — customization framing
- "Claim [X]% Off" — urgency + specificity
- "Start Your [Free] Trial" — low-risk entry point for subscription

## Zero-risk copy adjacent to CTAs

These short lines appear directly below or beside the CTA button. They dramatically reduce hesitation.

| Context | Zero-risk line |
|---------|---------------|
| Form with phone number | "We'll only call to discuss your quote. No spam." |
| Free quote form | "No credit card required. No commitment." |
| Sign-up form | "Unsubscribe anytime. We never share your data." |
| Free consultation | "30-minute call. No sales pressure." |
| First-purchase | "Free returns within 30 days. No questions asked." |
| SaaS trial | "Cancel anytime. No credit card required." |
| eComm checkout | "Secure checkout. Your card info is never stored." |

## First-person vs. third-person CTA copy

Research by Michael Aagaard (Unbounce) and others shows that first-person CTA copy consistently outperforms third-person in A/B tests:

| Third-person (weaker) | First-person (stronger) |
|----------------------|------------------------|
| Get Your Free Trial | Get My Free Trial |
| Start Your Quote | Start My Quote |
| Find Your Location | Find My Location |
| Download Your Guide | Download My Guide |

The first-person framing creates mental ownership of the outcome before the click. Use it as the default for form CTAs and high-conversion pages. (Note: always A/B test — some audiences respond differently.)

## CTA length guidelines

- **Primary CTA (hero / above fold):** 3–6 words. Specific, action-forward.
- **Secondary CTA:** 2–4 words. Contrast with primary (lower commitment).
- **Form submit button:** 3–7 words. Outcome-focused, first-person preferred.
- **Inline CTAs (in body copy):** Can be longer if the surrounding context primes the action.

Avoid CTAs longer than 10 words — they become hesitation-causing. The user should be able to process the CTA instantly.
