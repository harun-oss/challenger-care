## Goal

Design user testing allows to get feedback from users similar to the target audience
and ask their opinions on designs before they're developed. This is especially helpful for clients
with low traffic where traditional A/B testing would take a long time to reach significance.
Testing designs early in the process can indicate whether a new test/design may be successful.

## Test Types

### Preference Test
A preference test shows chosen design options to a panel of users (usually 20). Each user selects
the best design option and provides feedback on what they liked about their chosen option and what
they disliked about the other option.

- **Participants**: 15–20 users
- **Questions**: 3–4 follow-up questions
- **Best for**: Comparing 2+ design options before building

### Design Survey Test
A design survey allows to show a single design and ask multiple specific questions to
get targeted feedback from users. This usually gathers insights about first impressions, likes and
dislikes, what specific content motivates them to take the next action, what changes to the design
would motivate them more, etc.

- **Participants**: 20 users
- **Questions**: 7–9 questions
- **Best for**: Validating a single design concept before development

## SOP Steps

1. **Duplicate a user testing insights deck** from the example decks above (Custom Cuff is newest)
2. **Login to lyssna.com** and Create a New Study (Test or Survey)
3. **Choose the test type** (Preference Test or Design Survey Test)
4. **Upload images** — JPEGs exported from Figma and compressed to reduce file size
5. **Add follow-up questions**:
   - 3–4 questions for Preference Tests
   - 7–9 questions for Design Surveys
6. **Select 'Save & Continue'** to recruit users from the Lyssna panel
7. **Select demographics** to target users more specifically
8. **Set participant count**:
   - Preference Tests: 15–20 users
   - Design Surveys: 20 users
9. **Wait for results** (a few hours to a day depending on targeting)
10. **Summarize insights** in Challenger deck

## Question Bank

**Source**: https://docs.google.com/document/d/1Eby9fBfJq5xN8t-ETuThviqXUT6S4-5ua66Cxq8DdYY

### Preference Testing Follow-up Questions
1. Why did you choose that design and what specifically do you like about the page?
2. What did you not like about the other design?
3. Is there anything unclear about X? Please explain your thoughts.
4. What changes to this page would motivate you more to buy the product?
5. Based on this page, what do you think this product is?

### Design Survey Testing Questions
1. What are your first impressions of the product and brand?
2. Based on this page, what do you think this product is?
3. What specific qualities of this page design do you **like**, and why?
4. What specific qualities of this page design do you **dislike**, and why?
5. Would you want to book a free demo based on the information provided on this page? If yes, what specific content piqued your interest? If no, what content dissuaded you from wanting to book a demo?
6. What specific content and images on the page motivate you to X?
7. What changes to this page would motivate you more to X?
8. How easy or difficult is it for you to find the right product for your needs? Please explain.
9. Overall, on a scale of 1-10, how well do you like this page?
