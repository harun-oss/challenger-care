# Leverage Point Framework Reference

## The 7 Leverage Points

Every business has 7 leverage points that determine growth velocity and profitability. They operate in layers — you can't skip ahead.

### The Leverage Point Pyramid

```
Layer 4: EXPANSION    → Expand
Layer 3: ACQUISITION  → Reach + Convert
Layer 2: MONETIZATION → Money + Position
Layer 1: FOUNDATIONAL → Market + Product
```

Each layer must be solid before the one above it can work. Pouring money into ads (Reach) when the product doesn't retain (Product) is waste. Building a sales team (Expand) when there's no scalable acquisition channel (Reach) is premature.

---

## 1. MARKET

**The Question:** Do we serve a growing market with big budgets, and do they want to give us money?

**What an A looks like:** Large, growing market with clear budgets. Customers have funded line items for your category. Demand is inbound and increasing. Competitive landscape validates the market without being saturated.

**What an F looks like:** Shrinking or nonexistent market. Customers don't have budget. Heavy convincing required. No line item exists for what you sell.

**Grading Signals:**
- A: Market is massive, growing, and demand exceeds supply. Inbound interest increasing.
- B: Market is large and validated. Growth is clear. Some segments underserved.
- C: Market exists but is unclear in size. Budget allocation uncertain. Mixed signals on growth.
- D: Market is niche or stagnant. Budget is discretionary and easily cut. Heavy education required.
- F: Market doesn't exist, is actively shrinking, or customers don't see the problem as worth solving.

**Core Interview Questions:**
1. How big is your addressable market? How do you think about TAM?
2. Is this market growing? What tailwinds are you riding?
3. Do target buyers have budget for this? Is it a funded line item or discretionary?
4. Is inbound demand increasing? What's lead quality trending?
5. Are you getting pulled into adjacent markets? Is that helping or distracting?

**Follow-Up Probes:**
- What happens to your sales when the economy contracts? Do you get cut?
- Who are the 3 largest competitors? What does their growth tell you about the market?
- If you closed your top 5 dream accounts, what would they look like?

## 2. PRODUCT

**The Question:** Do we have a high-margin product or service that can be delivered at scale?

**What an A looks like:** High-margin, scalable delivery. Customers love it and stay. Clear "magic moment" in onboarding. Product improves over time without proportional team growth.

**What an F looks like:** Low margins, unscalable delivery, high churn, customers tolerate rather than love the product. Significant manual effort per customer.

**Grading Signals:**
- A: High margins (70%+), strong retention, scalable delivery, customers evangelize.
- B: Good margins, good retention, some manual onboarding. Product-market fit validated.
- C: Margins are okay. Retention is mixed. Product works but doesn't delight. Onboarding is heavy.
- D: Margins are thin or negative. Churn is high. Product requires significant manual effort. Bugs or quality issues.
- F: Product doesn't work reliably, or delivering it costs more than it generates.

**Core Interview Questions:**
1. Where does the product stand today? What's the roadmap?
2. What do customers love most? What frustrates them?
3. What does onboarding look like? How long from sign-up to value?
4. Can you deliver at 10x customers without 10x team? What breaks?
5. What's the path from free trial / first touch to paying customer?

**Follow-Up Probes:**
- What's your "magic moment" — the point where a user becomes a lifer?
- How many customers are fully self-serve vs. requiring hand-holding?
- If you had to pick: fix the product or sell more, which moves the needle more?

## 3. MONEY

**The Question:** Do we have pricing power? Do our price and margins allow us to be profitable while investing in growth?

**What an A looks like:** Strong pricing power, healthy margins, predictable cash flow. CAC payback under 6 months. Clear expansion revenue. Unit economics fund growth without external capital.

**What an F looks like:** No pricing power, thin or negative margins, unpredictable cash flow. CAC exceeds LTV. No path to profitability without fundamental changes.

**Grading Signals:**
- A: Pricing power, margins fund growth, CAC payback <6 months, expansion revenue, cash-positive.
- B: Good margins, predictable revenue. CAC payback <12 months. Some pricing flexibility.
- C: Margins are workable but not funding growth. CAC payback is long or unknown. Pricing feels capped.
- D: Margins are tight. CAC is uncertain. Cash flow is unpredictable. Pricing changes frequently.
- F: Burning cash with no path to unit economics working. Pricing is a race to the bottom.

**Core Interview Questions:**
1. Walk us through unit economics: average deal size, CAC, LTV, payback period.
2. Do you have pricing power, or are you competing on price?
3. What does your cash conversion cycle look like?
4. What's the expansion revenue opportunity?
5. How does pricing work differently across segments?

**Follow-Up Probes:**
- If you raised prices 20% tomorrow, what would happen?
- What's your gross margin per customer in year 1 vs. year 2?
- Do you know your CAC by channel? Which channel is most efficient?

## 4. POSITION

**The Question:** Are we clearly differentiated from competitors? Are we memorable and non-generic?

**What an A looks like:** Owns a category or niche. Instantly recognizable. Clear "why us" that prospects remember. Competitors can't easily replicate the positioning.

**What an F looks like:** Indistinguishable from competitors. No clear "why us." Prospects constantly confuse you with alternatives. Competing on price or features, not story.

**Grading Signals:**
- A: Owns a category. Brand is memorable. "Why us" is obvious and defensible. Competitors react to you.
- B: Strong differentiation assets. Clear story. But not yet "owning" a category. Some messaging inconsistency.
- C: Some differentiation but not consistently communicated. Prospects sometimes confuse you with competitors.
- D: Weak differentiation. Competing mostly on price or features. Messaging is generic.
- F: No clear positioning. Indistinguishable from alternatives. Brand is invisible.

**Core Interview Questions:**
1. When someone asks "what makes you different?" what do you say? What resonates?
2. How do customers describe you to their colleagues?
3. Do you own a category or are you fighting for space in an existing one?
4. If I visited your 5 closest competitors' websites, could I tell you apart in 5 seconds?
5. What does your brand voice sound like? What should it feel like?

**Follow-Up Probes:**
- What's the #1 objection you hear in sales? What does that tell you about positioning?
- If you had to pick one word/phrase for prospects to associate with you, what is it?
- Is anyone else trying to own the same positioning? Who's your positioning competitor?

## 5. REACH

**The Question:** Do we generate lots of profitable traffic from multiple scalable sources?

**What an A looks like:** Multiple scalable, profitable traffic sources. Predictable lead flow. Not dependent on any single channel. Organic and paid working together. CAC is known and manageable.

**What an F looks like:** No predictable lead source. All leads are founder-generated. No paid infrastructure. No organic engine. Dependent on word-of-mouth or one-off events.

**Grading Signals:**
- A: 3+ scalable channels generating profitable leads. Known CAC by channel. Predictable pipeline.
- B: 1-2 strong channels, others emerging. Some predictability. CAC is known but could be better.
- C: Leads are coming but inconsistently. Channels are partially built. Attribution is unclear. CPL/CPD volatile.
- D: Most leads are manual or event-driven. Paid is running but unpredictable. Organic is minimal. No attribution.
- F: All leads are founder-hustle. No scalable channels. No paid infrastructure. No content engine.

**Core Interview Questions:**
1. If you turned off all marketing tomorrow, how many leads would you still get? From where?
2. What channels drive the most qualified leads right now?
3. How is content/social performing? What's converting vs. just getting views?
4. What does the paid advertising picture look like across channels?
5. How dependent are you on any single channel?

**Follow-Up Probes:**
- What happens if you 10x your ad budget tomorrow? Would it work?
- What's your best-performing ad/content/campaign ever? Why did it work?
- Where are your ICP prospects hanging out that you're not present?

## 6. CONVERT

**The Question:** Do we have a sales process that is impressive — minimal drop-off or friction?

**What an A looks like:** Smooth, fast sales process. High close rate. Minimal friction. Self-serve option available. Post-sale onboarding is seamless. Every lead gets a structured follow-up.

**What an F looks like:** Long, messy sales process. Low close rate. Lots of drop-off. No self-serve. Leads go into a follow-up void. No post-demo nurture.

**Grading Signals:**
- A: 40%+ close rate. Fast cycle. Self-serve available. Structured nurture. Smooth onboarding.
- B: 20-40% close rate. Reasonable cycle. Good demo. Some follow-up gaps. Onboarding mostly works.
- C: 15-25% close rate with issues. Demo is good but everything else leaks. No nurture sequence. Onboarding is manual.
- D: Close rate declining. Long cycle. Lots of stalls. No self-serve. No follow-up system. Activation is a problem.
- F: Minimal conversion infrastructure. No pipeline tracking. No nurture. Leads are lost.

**Core Interview Questions:**
1. Walk through a winning deal from first touch to signed contract.
2. What's your close rate? How has it changed recently?
3. Where do prospects drop off or stall?
4. Can someone buy without talking to a human?
5. What happens after a prospect says "let me think about it" or "I'll check with my team"?

**Follow-Up Probes:**
- What's your no-show rate for demos?
- Do you give prospects tools to sell internally? (One-pagers, ROI calculators, etc.)
- What's the #1 reason deals stall? What's the #1 reason they close?

## 7. EXPAND

**The Question:** Are we ready for the next level of leverage — team, system, asset, and/or channel leverage?

**What an A looks like:** Business runs without founders in critical path. Systems and processes are documented. Team can execute independently. Growth doesn't require proportional effort increase.

**What an F looks like:** Everything depends on the founders. No documentation, no systems, no delegation. Growth requires proportional (or more) effort increase.

**Grading Signals:**
- A: Founder-free operations. Documented playbooks. Team executes independently. Systems scale.
- B: Key systems in place. Team handles most operations. Founders involved in strategy, not execution.
- C: Some systems exist. Founders still in most processes. Growth is possible but effortful.
- D: Founders do everything. No documentation. No systems. Growth = more founder hours.
- F: Founders are the only ones who know how anything works. Single point of failure on everything.

**Core Interview Questions:**
1. What's the next hire that would unlock the most growth?
2. What processes are manual today that should be automated?
3. Where are the biggest bottlenecks slowing execution?
4. What does the next 12 months look like if everything goes right?
5. What does your business look like when it runs without the founders in every process?

**Follow-Up Probes:**
- If you went on vacation for 2 weeks, what would break?
- What's the one thing you're doing that you should stop doing?
- For fundraising: what story do you want to tell? What metrics matter?
