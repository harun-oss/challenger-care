# Ad Copy Frameworks Reference

Reference for the meta-ads-copywriting skill. Read the relevant section during Phase 3A, 3B, or 3C.

---

## Primary Text Angles

Five distinct angles for the 5 primary texts. Each should produce a different emotional entry point to the same product.

### Angle 1: Pain-Point

Open by naming a frustration the audience knows intimately. The reader thinks "that's exactly me."

Pattern: `Tired of [problem]? [Brand] was built for this.`

Example (skincare):
```
Tired of spending $200 on products that do nothing for your skin? 🌿

Most skincare lines are 90% water and 10% marketing. [Brand] flips that. Every formula is packed with clinically-proven actives — no fillers, no fragrance, no nonsense.

See the difference in 30 days or your money back. Shop now →
```

### Angle 2: Aspirational

Open by painting the life the audience wants. Sell the transformation, not the product.

Pattern: `Imagine [desired outcome]. [Product] gets you there.`

Example (fitness supplement):
```
Imagine waking up actually energised — no coffee crash, no mid-afternoon slump. ⚡

[Brand] is the clean pre-workout formula trusted by 50,000+ athletes who refuse to compromise on what goes in their body. No artificial colours. No junk fillers. Just performance.

Your best session starts here. Try it risk-free today.
```

### Angle 3: Social Proof

Open with a number, a quote, or a crowd. Let other people do the convincing.

Pattern: `[X] people have already [done X]. Here's why.`

Example (home goods):
```
47,000 customers switched their bedding last year — and most said they wished they'd done it sooner. 😴

[Brand] sheets are made from 100% organic bamboo. Naturally cooling, hypoallergenic, and softer than anything you've slept on. Verified 4.9 stars across 12,000+ reviews.

Join them. Free shipping + 30-night sleep trial.
```

### Angle 4: Curiosity

Open with a question or an unexpected claim that creates a loop the reader has to close.

Pattern: `What if [unexpected idea]?` or `Most people don't know that [surprising fact].`

Example (insurance):
```
What if your current car insurance is costing you $600 more a year than it should? 🤔

Most drivers don't realise they're overpaying — until they spend 2 minutes comparing. [Brand] finds the gaps and gets you back to a rate that makes sense.

Check your rate now. No commitment, no calls, instant results.
```

### Angle 5: Urgency / FOMO

Open by making the cost of inaction clear. Time or scarcity creates the motivation to act now.

Pattern: `[Limited time / limited stock / deadline] — don't miss [outcome].`

Example (DTC fashion):
```
Only 48 hours left on our biggest sale of the year. ⏰

[Brand]'s summer edit is 30% off — the linen sets, the resort dresses, the pieces that sell out every season. These aren't restocked. When they're gone, they're gone.

Shop the sale before it closes. Free returns on every order.
```

## Headline Frameworks

Pattern examples for each of the 12 frameworks. All examples are within the 30–40 character limit.

| Framework | Pattern | Example |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| Benefits Driven | [Outcome] Without [Drawback] | "Glowing Skin, No Harsh Chemicals" |
| Problem-Solution | [Problem]? [Solution]. | "Oily Skin? Try Our Matte Formula" |
| Curiosity-Based | Question that creates a loop | "What Are You Really Washing With?" |
| FOMO | [Scarcity] + [Desirable outcome] | "72-Hour Sale — Don't Miss Out" |
| Testimonial-Style | Quote-adjacent social proof | "My Skin Has Never Looked Better" |
| How-To / Educational | How to [achieve X] | "How to Sleep Better in 7 Days" |
| Challenge-Based | Challenge the norm | "Stop Wasting Money on Moisturiser" |
| Shock / Controversy | Unexpected claim | "Your Mattress May Be the Problem" |
| Social Proof / Popularity | Numbers + outcome | "50,000 Athletes Trust This Formula" |
| Time-Sensitive / Urgency | Deadline + offer | "Sale Ends Tonight — Shop Now" |
| Authority / Expert-Backed | Credibility signal | "Dermatologist-Tested. Proven Safe." |
| Aspirational | Desired identity | "Dress Like You Mean It" |

**Character counting tip:** Count spaces. "Glowing Skin, No Harsh Chemicals" = 33 chars including the comma and space. Always count manually before finalising.

## Video Script Frameworks

Structure patterns for each of the 12 frameworks. Apply these to the 3-section (Hook / Body / CTA) format.

### 1. Problem-Agitate-Solution (PAS)
- **Hook:** State the problem sharply — make it sting
- **Body:** Agitate it (make the viewer feel the cost of not solving it), then introduce the solution
- **CTA:** Call to action tied to the relief the solution provides

### 2. Hook-Story-Offer (HSO)
- **Hook:** Disrupt with a bold statement or visual
- **Body:** Tell a short personal story (founder, customer, or character) that builds connection
- **CTA:** Introduce the offer as the natural conclusion to the story

### 3. Attention-Interest-Desire-Action (AIDA)
- **Hook:** Grab attention with something visually or verbally unexpected
- **Body:** Build interest with facts/features, then desire with benefits and emotion
- **CTA:** Clear action step

### 4. Before-After-Bridge (BAB)
- **Hook:** Show the "Before" state — the struggle
- **Body:** Show the "After" state — the transformation — then bridge to how the product makes it possible
- **CTA:** Invite the viewer to start their own transformation

### 5. Problem-Solution-Outcome (PSO)
- **Hook:** Name the problem in one sentence
- **Body:** Present the solution and explain how it works, then show the outcome (results, testimonials, proof)
- **CTA:** Tie the CTA to the specific outcome shown

### 6. Gain-Logic-Fear (GLF)
- **Hook:** Lead with the gain (what they'll get)
- **Body:** Provide the logical case (features, specs, proof), then introduce the fear of missing out
- **CTA:** Remove the fear — offer a guarantee, trial, or no-risk step

### 7. Star-Story-Solution (SSS)
- **Hook:** Introduce the "Star" — a relatable character (customer or persona)
- **Body:** Tell their story — the struggle, the journey
- **CTA:** Present the product as the solution that changed their story

### 8. Features-Advantages-Benefits (FAB)
- **Hook:** Lead with the most impressive feature, stated boldly
- **Body:** Explain the advantage this feature provides, then the real-world benefit to the viewer
- **CTA:** Make the benefit the reason to buy now

### 9. Open-Loop Curiosity (OLC)
- **Hook:** Open a question or mystery that can't be resolved without watching
- **Body:** Slowly reveal the answer while building the case for the product
- **CTA:** Close the loop with the product as the answer

### 10. Problem-Promise-Proof-Proposal (4Ps)
- **Hook:** State the problem
- **Body:** Make the promise (we can fix it), show proof (testimonial, stat, demo), then make the proposal
- **CTA:** The proposal IS the CTA — specific next step

### 11. Challenge-Solution-Transformation (CST)
- **Hook:** Name the challenge the viewer faces in their life right now
- **Body:** Present the solution and show the transformation it creates
- **CTA:** Invite the viewer to start the transformation

### 12. Fear-Doubt-Confidence (FDC)
- **Hook:** Surface the fear (something the viewer is worried about)
- **Body:** Acknowledge the doubt (validate their hesitation), then build confidence (proof, guarantee, social proof)
- **CTA:** Reinforce confidence — make the CTA feel safe and logical

## Vertical-Specific Defaults

When the user says "you pick the framework," use these defaults:

| Vertical | Primary Text Angle Variety | Recommended Headline Framework | Recommended Video Framework |
|---------|--------------------------|-------------------------------|----------------------------|
| eCommerce (fashion/home) | All 5 angles | Benefits Driven or FOMO | Before-After-Bridge (BAB) |
| eCommerce (health/beauty) | Pain-Point lead, Aspiration second | Aspirational or Social Proof | Hook-Story-Offer (HSO) |
| SaaS / Lead Gen | Problem-Solution lead | Problem-Solution | Problem-Agitate-Solution (PAS) |
| Health & Wellness | Aspirational lead, Pain-Point second | Aspirational or Authority | Star-Story-Solution (SSS) |
| Finance / Insurance | Pain-Point or Curiosity lead | Curiosity-Based or Problem-Solution | Gain-Logic-Fear (GLF) |
| Pet / Family | Social Proof or Aspiration lead | Social Proof / Popularity | Hook-Story-Offer (HSO) |
